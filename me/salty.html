Features

  • Choose from 12 fully-voiced episodes, or randomly generate a surprise!
  • A sassy host narrates over 150 (planned) unique questions!
  • Use a Lifesaver to turn a 4-choice question into a 2-choice one.
  • Up to 8 players play on a keyboard, a controller, a touchscreen, or even a phone!
  • Original music for every occasion!
  • 7 question types!
    • Standard — 4 choices, only one of which is right. If you answer right, you win money! If you answer wrong, you hear a zinger from the host. And lose money.
    • Candy Trivia — Worth a bit more! The question is about candy, or a dumb joke on the back of a wrapper.
    • Sorta Kinda — Quickly sort 7 items into one of two categories, sometimes even both of them!
    • All Outta Salt — The host gives you a silly phrase, whose syllables rhyme* with another more popular phrase. Buzz in quickly for more cash, but if you’re stumped, wait for the three clues.
      *Phrases may not perfectly rhyme in your accent.
    • Thousand-Question Question — Starts at 1000 times the normal prize! But with every bit of time that passes, the prize whittles down to zero. Only the first one to get it right will win the money.
    • Rage Against the Times — Ozzy, “our resident squatter”, is an old man who’s always angry about one thing or another. Find out why he’s mad this time.
    • Sugar Rush — A spooky speed round where you select only the options that match a description, based on the topic given at the start of the round.
    • Like It or Leave It — An action-packed final round where you “Like” items that match a clue, but “Leave” everything else. There are 4 items, the last of which changes with every clue!

Control schemes

Up to 8 contestants can play, mixing-and-matching the following control schemes:

  • Phones as controllers!
  • 2 players share a game controller
  • 4 players huddle up on a keyboard
  • 1 player using the mouse or touchscreen

Up to 100 audience members can play along and have their score tracked.

Platforms

Salty Trivia is available on the following platforms:

Browser - Play live on itch.io. This option is not as performant as the others, but is compatible with the most devices.
Windows executable - Play as a standalone application on your Windows computer. No need to install, just download, extract, and run.
Linux executable - Play as a standalone application on your Linux computer. No need to install, just download, extract, and run.
Android app - Install the .APK file as an app on your Android phone. (You will need to enable “Install from unknown sources” for this.)

You can download all the non-browser versions from the itch.io listing.

  • “Open source”: To achieve the first and third freedoms, the game’s code is open-source, which means all the code that makes up the game is available to see, copy, and modify online (on GitHub in this case). In addition, it uses the Godot game engine, which is similarly free and open source.

However, there are some restrictions. You may not use the code to impersonate us, or otherwise scam people. The recordings of our voices are available for ease of modification, but if you release a modification, you must replace all the provided voice files.
